# AI Image Attribution via Dual-Fusion Models

This repository contains the code and research paper for a dual-stream neural architecture designed to trace AI-generated images back to their specific source models (e.g., Stable Diffusion, Midjourney, DALL-E). This was developed as a final project for Stanford's CS229 (Machine Learning).

While binary real-vs-fake detection is fairly robust, attributing an image to a specific generative model often fails when images undergo standard real-world post-processing like compression or resizing. To tackle this, we built a hybrid model that analyzes both the spatial (RGB) pixels and the underlying frequency (FFT) fingerprints left behind by image generators.

## Repository Contents

* **`Dual-fusion Models for AI Image Attribution.pdf`**: The full research paper detailing our architecture, experimental setup on the WILD dataset, and final results.
* **`Project_code.ipynb`**: The complete Jupyter Notebook containing data preprocessing, synthetic image generation, model architectures, and SHAP-based feature importance evaluation.

## Datasets

Our models were trained and tested on two primary data sources:

* **The WILD Dataset**: A closed set of 10,000 high-resolution (1024x1024) images of faces generated by 10 of the most popular commercial and open-source models (including Midjourney, DALL-E 3, and Adobe Firefly). This dataset also includes post-processed variants (Steps 1-3) featuring random crops, compression, and blurring to simulate real-world environmental degradation.
* **Custom "Not Faces" Dataset**: To evaluate how well our models generalized beyond facial structures, we synthetically generated an additional 200 images using Stable Diffusion 3.5 and Stable Diffusion XL (100 images each). These images utilized highly diverse, non-facial prompts (e.g., "A panoramic view of a megacity at night") to ensure the networks were learning true generator fingerprints rather than simply overfitting to semantic content.

## Methodology

Our pipeline processes images through two parallel branches before fusing them for a final prediction:

* **Spatial Stream (RGB)**: Uses a pre-trained Vision Transformer (ViT-B/16) with frozen weights to extract robust global features from the spatial domain.
* **Frequency Stream (FFT)**: Takes a 2D Fast Fourier Transform of the image and passes it through a ResNet-50 trained from scratch to catch granular spectral artifacts.
* **Fusion Head**: The embeddings from both streams are concatenated and passed through a multi-layer perceptron (MLP) classification head to output the final generator prediction.

## Key Findings

* **Strong Baseline Performance**: The dual-stream models performed exceptionally well on clean test sets, accurately identifying the source generator with high balanced accuracy.
* **Fragility to Post-Processing**: Performance dropped sharply when images were subjected to realistic distortions, showing that high-frequency generator fingerprints are highly fragile in the wild.
* **Feature Importance**: Our SHAP value analysis confirmed that the model didn't over-rely on the fragile frequency domain, pulling roughly 47.3% of its predictive power from the spatial RGB stream and 52.7% from the frequency stream.
